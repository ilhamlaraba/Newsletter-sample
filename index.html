Step-up with these 3 yoga basics

3 Fundamental basics to enjoy a safe and beneficial yoga practice.

Don't ignore your pain

The quotation "No pain, no gain" wasn't made for yoga since yoga is about listening to your body and responding accordingly.

Naturally, holding yoga poses causes muscle contractions that result in micro-tears to the tissue, and pushing through the pain and ignoring it is a poor concept, but giving your tissues time to recover and using stretching exercises is the most suitable choice. So practice with more awareness and care.

Don't move to the next level.

Remember, the prize is to develop flexibility, strength, and body awareness.

Pushing your body to levels you are not ready to go to means setting yourself up for injuries.

So, simply pay attention to your teacher and your own body, and don't push yourself.

Don't forget to breath

While you strive to balance and ensure the perfect alignment to get the post right, Irregular or incorrect breathing starts to hit, and a gentle reminder from your teacher is not enough.

The priority is to keep an eye on your breathing and take the time to get your breath under control during your practice.
